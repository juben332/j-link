The Spot Latte

Double shot of single-origin espresso pulled on a La Marzocco, finished with microfoam steamed to 65°C. Dialled fresh each morning — extraction sits between 27–30 seconds. The latte art isn't decoration; it's how we know the milk is right.

Mushroom Eggs Benedict

Two poached eggs on toasted brioche, stacked with sautéed wild mushrooms and hollandaise made fresh each service — no shortcuts, 63°C yolks. First version used packaged brioche; switched to house-baked after the third revision. Current plating took four tries to get the mushroom layering right.

Tiramisu

Ladyfingers soaked in our own espresso, layered with mascarpone cream and finished with 72% cacao. Set overnight — minimum 12 hours. We tried rushing it once for a busy weekend; the texture wasn't right and we pulled it. Ships when it's ready.

Sec. 03 — About

The Spot started with one question: why does specialty coffee have to feel pretentious? We wanted a place where the coffee is taken seriously but the experience isn't — somewhere you sit for two hours, order twice, and leave knowing exactly what you drank.

Every bean on the menu is single-origin. Every espresso is dialled fresh at open. The food was built around the coffee — not the other way around. Hollandaise from scratch, batter rested, tiramisu set overnight. Short menu, every item worth ordering.

Opened in 2022. Iterated since. The current space — white marble, warm timber, clean lines — is version three of the fit-out. First two were close but not right. This one is.
